Degree Programs

Explore our diverse range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs

Empowering the Next Generation of Innovators

The Faculty of Science at the University of Ruhuna offers a diverse ecosystem of undergraduate and postgraduate programs. Whether you are looking to decode the mysteries of the natural world or build the next great software architecture, our curriculum is built to bridge the gap between academic theory and real-world application.

BCS General Degree (Bachelor of Computer Science)

Duration: 3 Years

Overview: This program provides a broad foundation in computing, focusing on the core principles of software development, database management, and networking. It is designed for students who want a versatile skill set to enter the tech industry quickly.

Key Focus: Software engineering basics, web technologies, and systems analysis.

BCS Special Degree (Bachelor of Computer Science Honours)

Duration: 4 Years

Overview: An intensive, research-oriented program for students seeking in-depth expertise. It includes advanced topics like Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, and Cybersecurity. The final year involves a significant individual research project or industrial placement.

Key Focus: Advanced algorithms, machine learning, and specialized technical innovation

BSc General Degree (Bachelor of Science)

Duration: 3 Years

Overview: This flexible degree allows students to combine Computer Science subjects (such as Mathematics, Physics, or Statistics). It is ideal for interdisciplinary education and plan to apply computing skills in scientific or analytical fields.

Key Focus: Interdisciplinary science, logic, and computational problem-solving.

BSc Special Degree (Bachelor of Science Honours in Computer Science)

Duration: 4 Years

Overview: This is a prestigious pathway for students who excel in their first two years. It focuses on the theoretical and mathematical foundations of computing. It prepares students for academic careers, high-level software architecture, or specialized scientific research.

Key Focus: Theory of computation, high-performance computing, and artificial intelligence research.
