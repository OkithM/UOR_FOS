Departments

Explore our departments of Faculty of Science, University of Ruhuna

Department of Botany

The Department of Botany conducts courses in all major fields of Botany for undergraduate students registered for B.Sc. General Degree and B.Sc. Special Degree programmes. In addition to this, research facilities are offered to those students seeking postgraduate qualifications such as M.Sc., M.Phil., and Ph.D. Degrees in Botany and related fields. The Department has the following infrastructure facilities: two large elementary laboratories to cater up to 130 undergraduate students, two well-equipped research laboratories, one taxonomy laboratory with the Departmental herbarium, one well-equipped molecular biology laboratory, two greenhouses, one lecture theater, a seminar room, and a tissue culture lab.

Department of Chemistry

The Department of Chemistry offers a robust curriculum in organic, inorganic, physical, and analytical chemistry for B.Sc. General and Special Degree students. Postgraduate research is supported in areas such as spectroscopy, environmental chemistry, and nanomaterials, leading to M.Sc., M.Phil., and Ph.D. degrees. Facilities include multiple teaching laboratories, advanced instrumentation labs (including NMR and GC-MS), a chemical storage unit, a seminar room, and a dedicated postgraduate research wing.

Department of Computer Science

The Department of Computer Science delivers courses in programming, algorithms, data structures, software engineering, and artificial intelligence for B.Sc. General and Special Degree students. Postgraduate programs include M.Sc., M.Phil., and Ph.D. degrees in areas such as machine learning, cybersecurity, and data science. The department features multiple computer labs with high-speed internet, a server room, smart classrooms, and collaborative spaces for student projects and research.

Department of Mathematics

The Department of Mathematics offers a wide range of courses in pure and applied mathematics for students enrolled in B.Sc. General and Special Degree programmes. Postgraduate research is available in mathematical modeling, statistics, and computational mathematics, leading to M.Sc., M.Phil., and Ph.D. degrees. Facilities include a mathematics resource center, seminar halls, a computer-aided mathematics lab, and a departmental library with access to international journals.

Department of Physics

The Department of Physics provides foundational and advanced courses in classical mechanics, electromagnetism, quantum physics, and thermodynamics for B.Sc. General and Special Degree students. Postgraduate programs include M.Sc., M.Phil., and Ph.D. degrees in theoretical and applied physics. The department houses modern physics laboratories, an optics lab, electronics lab, computational physics lab, and an observatory equipped for astronomical research.

Department of Zoology

The Department of Zoology offers comprehensive courses in animal biology, ecology, and physiology for students pursuing B.Sc. General and Special Degree programmes. Postgraduate research opportunities are available in areas such as wildlife biology, entomology, and conservation science, leading to M.Sc., M.Phil., and Ph.D. qualifications. Facilities include two undergraduate teaching laboratories, a dedicated research lab for field biology, a museum with preserved specimens, a seminar room, and access to field stations for ecological studies.
